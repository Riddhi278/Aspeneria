    Symptoms of PCOS

           

                Different people have different symptoms of PCOS. Some people do not have any signs or symptoms at all.        

           

    Some warning signs of PCOS are as follows :

           
  • 1) Irregular Periods- Having few menstural periods or having periods that aren't regular are common signs of PCOS.
    •        
  • 2) Too much Androgrn- High level of the hormone androgen may result in excess facial and body hair.
    •        
  • 3) Polycystic ovaries- Your ovaries might be bigger.
    •        
  • PCOS signs and symptoms are typically more severe in people with Obesity

    How Is PCOS Treated?

           

    There is no single test to specifically diagnose Polycystic ovary Syndrome(PCOS).             A physical exam includes checking for signs of excess hair growth, insulin resistance and acne.        

           
  • Your health care provider might then recommend:
    •        
                 
    1. Pelvic Exam : During a Pelvic exam, your provider can check your reproductive organs for masses,growth or other changes.
      2.            
    2. Blood Test : Blood test can measure hormone levels. This testing can exclude possible causes of menstural problems or androgen excess that mimic PCOS.
      3.            
    3. Ultrasound : An ultrasound can check the appearance of your ovaries and the thickness of the lining of your uterus.

    Difference between PCOD and PCOS

           

                Some females might be confused whether PCOD and PCOS are the same or different from each other. Both the medical conditions are associated with ovaries and hormonal imbalance in women during their reproductive age (between 12 and 51 years) and depicts the same symptoms        

                     

           

  • 1)PCOD doesn't have any serious complications while PCOS have serious complications such as type 2             diabetes, heart disease, high-blood pressure and endometrial cancer in later stage..   
    •        
  • 2)PCOD doesn't affect fertility in women, in this condition woman still can ovulate and become pregnant with little help, following medication can complete pregnancy.              PCOS seriously affects fertility in women. Due to PCOS woman cannot ovulate regularly, making them hard to get pregnant.
    •        
  • 3)PCOD is a condition in which ovaries produce many immature or partially mature eggs, this happen due to poor lifestyle, obesity, stress and hormonal imbalance.               PCOS is a metabolic disorder and more severe form of PCOD can lead to anovulation where ovaries stop releasing eggs.
